Personal vs billing details for personal invoices

Comparison of the details captured under the personal address against the institutional billing address for unique BillingDetailsRecorded records.

556

unique billing-detail records reviewed after removing exact duplicates from a 3,498-row source file.

Scope and method

What was compared

  • Filtered the CSV to BillingDetailsRecorded only.
  • Removed exact duplicate rows before analysing similarity.
  • Compared personal and billing values across city, phone, street, country, house number, postal code and email.
  • Used the supplied boolean equality flags and checked where formatting likely explains the difference.

Headline result

Most records are flagged different, but that overstates how often the data truly diverges

Address flag = false

321 of 556 records say the personal and billing address are not equal.

Address flag = true

The key analytical question is not just whether the address flag is false, but whether the underlying field differences are substantive or mostly formatting, casing, institutional context, or partial address enrichment.

Where mismatch sits

Most disagreement sits in street text and contact details, not country

Country almost always matches and city usually does, which suggests many mismatches reflect local formatting, institutional-detail enrichment, or alternate contact details rather than a wholly different billing location.

Interpretation

If country, email, city and postcode all line up, a false address flag often reflects notation differences, missing apartment information, or a billing address enriched with department or campus text.
